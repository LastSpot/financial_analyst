# The 3-Signal Confirmation Rule

**Hedge only when 2 of these 3 signals occur simultaneously.**

---

## Signal 1 — Volatility spike

- **Condition:** VIX &gt; 20 and rising.
- **Meaning:** Indicates market stress.

## Signal 2 — Market trend weakening

- **Check:** Invesco QQQ Trust and SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust.
- **Condition:** Price falls below the 50-day moving average.
- **Meaning:** Momentum is weakening.

## Signal 3 — Liquidity tightening

- **Watch:** U.S. Treasury yields (especially the 10-year).
- **Condition:** 10-year yield rises rapidly.
- **Meaning:** Growth stocks often fall; they are sensitive to discount-rate changes.

---

## Practical decision matrix

| Signals triggered | Action        |
|-------------------|---------------|
| 0                 | No hedge      |
| 1                 | Watch closely |
| 2                 | Hedge         |
| 3                 | Strong hedge  |

---

## Example scenario

**Setup:**

- VIX jumps from 14 → 22  
- QQQ still trending upward  
- Bond yields stable  

**Result:** Do nothing. This is likely a temporary volatility spike; only one signal is triggered.
